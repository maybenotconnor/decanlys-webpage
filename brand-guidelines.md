# Decanlys Brand Guidelines

## Brand Overview

Decanlys is Connor's solo AI consulting practice serving Connecticut businesses. The brand speaks in first person ("I" / "my") — this is one person's expertise, not a faceless agency.

**Personality:** Trustworthy, Clear, Capable.

**Emotional goal:** Relief + trust. Within 30 seconds, someone should feel: *"Finally, someone who gets it and can actually help."* They should feel safe handing over a problem.

**Tone:** Conversational but not casual — like a trusted advisor at a networking event. Speak to outcomes, not technology. Short sentences. Active voice. Direct.

---

## Target Audience

Connecticut business owners and decision-makers (SMB to enterprise) evaluating whether AI consulting is worth their time. They arrive skeptical of hype, short on patience, and looking for someone who "gets it." Their context: they've probably Googled "AI consulting Connecticut" or been referred by a peer. They need to quickly assess credibility and decide whether a 30-minute call is worth booking.

## Logo

### The Mark

The Decanlys mark is a **5-node interconnected network** — three nodes on the left, two on the right, connected by lines. It represents connection, structure, and the way AI links into existing workflows.

- **Node fill:** `#e1d5e7` (lavender)
- **Node/line stroke:** `#9673a6` (brand purple)
- **Stroke weight:** 2.5px at standard size, rounded caps
- **Node radius:** 4.5px at standard size

The mark is an SVG and should always be rendered as vector, never rasterized.

### The Wordmark

The word **decanlys** is set in **SUSE Bold (700)**, all lowercase, at 24px in the navigation. Color is `#8967A1` (primary accent) on light backgrounds, or `rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.9)` on dark backgrounds.

Letter-spacing: `0.01em`.

### Logo Usage

- The mark and wordmark appear together, side by side, with a 12px gap.
- The mark always appears to the left of the wordmark.
- Do not rotate, distort, or recolor the mark outside the specified palette.
- Do not place the logo on busy or low-contrast backgrounds.
- Minimum clear space around the logo: the width of one node (roughly 10px at standard size).

## Color Palette

| Role | Hex | Usage |
|------|-----|-------|
| **Background** | `#FFFFFF` | Primary canvas — the default for all pages and materials |
| **Primary accent** | `#8967A1` | Wordmark, headings, CTAs, links, key highlights. WCAG AA 4.65:1 on white |
| **Brand purple** | `#A680B8` | Logo SVG strokes and decorative elements only — not for text or UI |
| **Light fill** | `#e1d5e7` | Node fills, soft backgrounds, cards, emphasis areas |
| **Section background** | `#F8F5FA` | Alternating section backgrounds — a lavender tint, not grey |
| **Stroke accent** | `#7E6090` | Labels, button hover states, secondary emphasis. WCAG AA 5.30:1 on white |
| **Brand stroke** | `#9673a6` | Logo SVG strokes only |
| **Body text** | `#2D2D2D` | All primary body text — warm dark, not harsh pure black |
| **Muted text** | `#6B6B6B` | Captions, labels, secondary info, supporting copy |

### Color Rules

- The palette is purple-forward. There are no blues, greens, or warm accent colors.
- `#8967A1` (primary accent) is the workhorse — use it for anything that needs to draw attention.
- `#A680B8` and `#9673a6` are reserved for the logo and decorative node graphics. Do not use them for text, buttons, or UI elements.
- Dark backgrounds use `#2D2D2D` (the body text color) as the fill, with white text at reduced opacity (`rgba(255, 255, 255, 0.7)` for body, `0.9` for emphasis).
- Never use pure black (`#000000`) or pure grey for text.

## Typography

**Typeface:** SUSE (Google Fonts) — geometric, slightly rounded, modern but approachable. Used throughout with no secondary typeface.

**Fallback stack:** `'SUSE', system-ui, -apple-system, sans-serif`

### Type Scale

| Role | Weight | Size | Line Height |
|------|--------|------|-------------|
| Hero headline | Bold (700) | 52px (desktop), 32px (mobile) | 1.15 |
| Section heading | SemiBold (600) | 36px (desktop), 28px (mobile) | 1.25 |
| Card/subsection heading | SemiBold (600) | 22px (desktop), 19px (mobile) | — |
| Body text | Regular (400) | 18px (desktop), 16px (mobile) | 1.65 |
| Section labels | Medium (500) | 14px | — |
| Muted/caption | Regular (400) | 14–16px | — |
| Wordmark | Bold (700) | 24px | — |

### Typography Rules

- Body line-height is generous at 1.65 — this is intentional, not an accident. It contributes to the spacious, calm feel.
- Section labels are uppercase with `letter-spacing: 0.15em`, set in the stroke accent color (`#7E6090`).
- Never use light or thin weights. The minimum weight is Regular (400).

## Voice & Tone

### Guiding Principles

- First person singular: "I build," "I'll handle it," "my approach" — never "we" or "our team."
- Speak to what the reader gets, not what the technology does.
- Short sentences. Short paragraphs. Say it and move on.
- Sound like a person, not a brochure.

### Words to Use

"save time," "get more done," "stop doing it manually," "I'll handle it," "practical," "clear path," "real results," "works for your team"

### Words to Avoid

"leverage," "synergy," "cutting-edge," "revolutionize," "local," "disrupt," "transform," "next-generation," "unlock," "empower," "scalable solution," "paradigm"

### Tone Examples

| Instead of | Write |
|------------|-------|
| "We leverage AI to optimize your business processes" | "I find the repetitive tasks eating your team's time and build systems that handle them" |
| "Our cutting-edge solutions drive transformation" | "AI should work for people, not the other way around" |
| "Unlock the power of artificial intelligence" | "Less busywork, faster decisions, happier teams" |

## Visual Motifs

### The Node Network

The 5-node network from the logo is the brand's visual throughline. It appears:

- **As the logo mark** — 3 left nodes, 2 right nodes, fully connected
- **As section dividers** — simplified to 3 nodes in a triangle formation (lavender fills, `#7E6090` strokes)
- **As a hero watermark** — an expanded, multi-column node network at 10% opacity as a subtle background element
- **As decorative accents** — small node clusters before section headings (like the diamond shape above the CTA)

All node graphics use the same visual language: **lavender fills (`#e1d5e7`), brand stroke outlines (`#9673a6` or `#7E6090`), rounded line caps, consistent stroke widths.**

### Section Dividers

Between major sections, a small 3-node SVG triangle serves as a visual break. It sits centered, at 50% opacity, with `#e1d5e7` fills and `#7E6090` strokes. This replaces traditional horizontal rules.

## Aesthetic Direction

- **Theme**: Light mode only. White foundation, purple accent palette, lavender soft fills.
- **Visual tone**: Clean, spacious, flat. No shadows on cards. No gradients. No parallax or animated backgrounds. Generous whitespace — it's part of the design.
- **Feel**: "A trusted advisor walked into the room" — not a tech demo.

---

## Design Principles

1. **Clarity over cleverness** — Every element communicates. Nothing merely decorates. If it doesn't help the visitor understand or take action, remove it.

2. **Spacious confidence** — Generous whitespace conveys authority and calm. Let the design breathe. Padded sections, short paragraphs, room between elements. White space is not empty space.

3. **Personal, not corporate** — This is one person's brand. Design should feel human and direct, never like a committee designed it.

4. **Substance over spectacle** — No tech theater. Subtle animations, flat cards, clean lines. The design should feel effortless, not impressive.

5. **Accessible by default** — WCAG AA contrast ratios, keyboard navigation, screen reader support, and `prefers-reduced-motion` respected. Good design serves everyone.

---

## Motion

Motion is subtle and purposeful. It signals that the page is alive without competing for attention.

- **Hero:** Staggered fade-up on page load, ~100ms delay between elements
- **Sections:** Gentle fade-up (24px travel, 0.6s duration) on scroll-into-view via Intersection Observer
- **Buttons:** Background color transition on hover (200ms ease)
- **Navigation:** Subtle drop shadow appears on scroll

### Motion Rules

- No parallax. No particle effects. No animated backgrounds.
- No bounce, slide-from-side, or elastic easing.
- All motion must respect `prefers-reduced-motion` — when enabled, all animations and transitions resolve instantly.

---

## Technical Constraints

- Static single-page site: HTML/CSS/vanilla JS
- No frameworks, no build tools
- Google Fonts (SUSE) as sole external dependency
- Optimized for fast load — no heavy assets
- All text selectable, all images with alt text, all interactions keyboard-accessible
- Responsive: desktop (1200+), tablet (768-1199), mobile (<768)

---

## What to Avoid

- **Silicon Valley startup aesthetics** — no dark mode hero sections, no gradient mesh backgrounds, no "built different" energy
- **Corporate brochure feel** — no stock photos of handshakes, no mission/vision/values grids, no "our team" carousel
- **AI jargon websites** — no particle effects, no neural network visualizations, no "powered by machine learning" badges
- **Visual noise** — no card shadows, no gradients on buttons, no decorative icons for the sake of decoration
- **Generic AI imagery** — no robot illustrations, no brain graphics, no circuit board patterns
